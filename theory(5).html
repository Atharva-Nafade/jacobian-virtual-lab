Theory of Jacobian Matrix and Jacobian Determinant

The Jacobian is a determinant formed using first-order partial derivatives of functions involving two or more variables. It is used in multivariable calculus and coordinate transformations.

Jacobian Matrix

The Jacobian matrix is a matrix of partial derivatives of functions.

J = | ∂x/∂u ∂x/∂v |
| ∂y/∂u ∂y/∂v |

Jacobian Determinant

J = ∂(x,y) / ∂(u,v)
J = (∂x/∂u)(∂y/∂v) - (∂x/∂v)(∂y/∂u)

Steps to Solve Jacobian

  • Write the functions clearly.
  • Find all partial derivatives.
  • Form the Jacobian matrix.
  • Find determinant of matrix.
  • Simplify the answer.

Important Property

J × J' = 1

where J' is the Jacobian of inverse transformation.

Applications

  • Coordinate transformation
  • Engineering mathematics
  • Multiple integrals
  • Area and volume transformations
